Why GeoSci

May 2, 2016

The geoscientific problems we are facing, from resource exploration to environmental management will require the integration of information and methods across the geoscience disciplines: geology, hydrogeology, geophysics, geochemistry, and geotechnical engineering. Integrating across these disciplines is a challenging task: each has different terminology, methods and workflows. We believe that the first step to integration is opening up communication lines. This is our aim with GeoSci.xyz.

We are starting with geophysics. Geophysics has not yet reached its full potential for its contribution to solving problems in the geosciences. However that is changing - today the combination of better field systems and high quality data, advances in scientific computing, and the exponential increase in computer power allows us to solve problems that were not possible a few years ago. It is becoming more commonplace to acquire laboratory or in-situ measurements of physical properties and these are essential links in tying the geophysics to questions about the earth. The other advance is the open-source technology associated with the internet which allows technical material and ideas to be generated by multiple contributors through sources like Github. The cloud- based computing environment and advanced software has also opened up new ways for interacting with equations and concepts in real time as material is being read or collaborative projects are pursued.

The goal of GeoSci.xyz is to be a portal for information and computational resources for geoscientists. It is modular in design and each component is open-source with content licensed under Creative Commons 4.0. Currently there are three modules linked with GeoSci.
